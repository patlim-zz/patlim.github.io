Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Understanding neuroplasticity can benefit those of the fixed mindset.

This is great for children, students or anyone really.

Poor math skills can be worked on and improved, your able to train and improve these skills.


Discuss how we might engage with the principals of neuroplasticity for your own benefit
- Firstly a mindset change, understanding that I can improve in my areas of weakness
- I need to practice and spend plenty of time at it
- I must care about what I’m training and maintain good habits


What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity
- Clarity - Important when starting out any behaviour change
- Practice and keep learning
- Set goals
- Build a routine
- Less stress
- Enough sleep
- Learn a language or instrument
- Eating fish & turmeric (why not both?)

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

Clarity, like I mentioned above, is important when starting out a new behaviour change.

In the same sense, people must first believe the growth mindset and that through practice and dedication, they can improve.


In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I am surprised by how clear the results are in schools and why it isn’t practiced everywhere. For me, I can be quite stubborn and think my weaknesses and my weaknesses. But I need to keep the mindset that these things can change and be improved.


How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

With every sprint and challenge, I need to come in believing I can take them on and succeed.

I need to take failures or disappointment as learning experience.

Do it for myself and cultivate a sense of purpose salience.

Don’t compare myself with others.

Lastly my favourite, replacing self doubt with the word “yet”.
