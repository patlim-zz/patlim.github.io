Emotional Intelligence

The ability to understand, use and manage your own emotions in positive ways. Such as relieving stress, communicating effectively, empathising with others, overcoming challenges and defusing conflict.

IQ - The ability to process information and come to sound decisions
EQ - the ability to process emotions - both others’ and your own, using them think and make sound decisions

EQ can be a more effective indicator of potential success. There has been correlation with EQ and leadership potential, employability, and even health. Emotional intelligence will distinguish you as an effective leader

IQ ability to learn, understand and apply information to skills, logical reasoning, word comprehension, math skills, abstract and spatial thinking, filter irrelevant info.
EQ actually forms the foundations for all your interactions/impulses, including learning process; thus effecting IQ and cognitive development.
From what I’ve read, it seems difficult to really say which is more important.
In workplace - EQ leads to better teamwork, leadership and successful relations.
Whereas with IQ, you’ll have success with challenging tasks, ability to analyse and connect dots, research and dev
The key is EQ can be improved and it exteremly important in career development, so improving those skills and attributes are extremely valuable.

The five core attributes of emotional intelligence
Self Awareness
- The ability to understand your effect on others, play to your strengths and admit your weaknesses
Empathy
- No just listening to others, but really trying to understand their point of view
Motivation
- Having the innate passion to challenge yourself and remain optimistic with the going gets tough
Self regulation/management
- Being able to control impulses and avoid acting rashly
Social Skills
- ability to manage relations and productively express your emotions

Ways to develop EQ
Practicing self awareness
Know what you’re doing (turn off auto-pilot)
Know what you’re feeling
know your own emotional bullshit

Channeling your emotions well
No such thing as a good or bad emotion - just good and bad reactions to your emotions

Learn to motivate yourself
“Do something principle” - taking action is not just the effect of motivation, but also the cause of it

Recognise emotions in others to create healthier relationships
Listen and share yourself honestly with others
You don’t necessarily have to understand others, but rather to accept them as they are, even the you don’t understand them.

Infuse your emotions with value
In order to live the life you truly want to live, be clear about what you truly value because that’s where your emotional energy will be directed.
Conmen are highly emotionally intelligent, so is a salesman. They value themselves above others.
